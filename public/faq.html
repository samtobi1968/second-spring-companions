Frequently Asked Questions

Answers, thoughtfully considered.

Everything you might want to know about how Second Spring works, who our Springers are, and what to expect from a companionship.

The Model

What exactly is a Springer?

A Springer is a warm, vetted companion matched with an older adult for friendship, conversation, and shared interests. Springers are not nurses, carers, or clinicians — they are thoughtful people who bring presence, curiosity, and consistency.

How does the matching process work?

It begins with a free consultation to understand interests, routines, and what a good Springer looks like for you. We then introduce a Springer whose personality and interests align with your loved one, followed by a relaxed meet-and-greet before regular visits begin at a pace that suits them.

What do Springers actually do during visits?

Visits are shaped by the individual — conversation over tea, a walk in the park, a museum trip, reading aloud, gardening, music, or simply quiet company. The aim is genuine connection, not a checklist.

Do you provide personal care or medical support?

No. Second Spring is a companionship registry only. We do not provide nursing, personal care, medication management, or any clinical assessments. Families requiring care should engage a regulated provider alongside us.

Safety & Trust

How are Springers vetted?

Every Springer holds an Enhanced DBS check, provides verified references, and completes an in-person interview with our Registrar. We accept a small fraction of applicants — character, warmth, and reliability matter as much as background.

Are Springers insured?

Yes. Second Spring carries public liability and professional indemnity insurance covering all introductions made through our registry.

What if the match isn't quite right?

It happens, and it's perfectly fine. Tell us and we'll re-match thoughtfully — at no additional cost. The relationship is everything; we'd rather take the time to get it right.

How do you protect privacy?

We are ICO registered and fully GDPR compliant. Personal information is shared only with the matched Springer and only to the extent needed to support the companionship.

Fees & Logistics

How much does it cost?

Companionship visits are £28 per hour. The initial consultation, matching, and meet-and-greet are complimentary — you only pay once regular visits begin.

How are visits scheduled and paid for?

Visits are scheduled directly between the family and the Springer at a cadence that suits everyone. We invoice monthly in arrears by bank transfer or card.

What if I need to cancel a visit?

Cancellations made with at least 24 hours' notice are free of charge. Inside 24 hours, the visit fee is payable in full to respect the Springer's time.

Where do you operate?

We serve families across the United Kingdom. Coverage is densest in and around major cities; tell us where you are and we'll let you know honestly whether we can match well in your area.

Have a specific question?

Our Registrar is happy to talk through anything not covered here.
